What is Nursing Care?


Nursing care is a specialized package of assistance provided by specially trained caregivers and overseen by a qualified clinical nurse. This type of care is typically more complex in nature, necessitating specialist support for specific health care needs. mportant to understand how it's linked to health.

Nursing care at home allows someone with a complex health condition to remain in their own home rather than moving into residential care. This allows them to retain the familiarity and safety of their own possessions and surroundings, as well as to continue living with a partner, spouse, or pet.

You can be confident that you will be in the hands of someone who genuinely cares about your emotional and physical well-being, aiming to keep you as comfortable as possible at home in the familiarity and safety of your own environment.

If you have a serious or terminal illness, nursing care or palliative care can allow you to spend your final months and days at home while receiving specialized, one-on-one care. Having palliative care at home means less disruption to your routines and care that is meaningful to you, all while maintaining your dignity and wellbeing.
